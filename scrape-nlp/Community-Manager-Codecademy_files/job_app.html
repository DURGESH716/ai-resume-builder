Hello, World! Codecademy has helped tens of millions of learners upgrade their careers, build meaningful projects and gain confidence in their skills with engaging, accessible, and flexible education on programming and data skills. We provide hands-on interactive lessons ranging from Python to R to JavaScript and everything in between. Our learners have gone on to start companies, new jobs, and new lives thanks to what they’ve learned with Codecademy, and we’re thrilled to take that impact to the next level.

Codecademy was started in 2011 by two college students in a dorm room at Columbia that were frustrated by the huge gap between education and employment. Almost a decade later, we are a growing, diverse team of 100+ headquartered in SoHo, NYC. We’ve raised over $40m in venture capital funding from top investors including Union Square Ventures, Kleiner Perkins, Naspers, Y Combinator, and more.

If you want to help build a business that impacts tens of millions of people each year and helps them lead better lives, join us! 

As a Community Manager you will build a global community of Codecademy chapters to facilitate collaborative learning. You’ll scale our chapters program worldwide, creating collaborative, productive, and positive communities of passionate Codecademy learners who wish to learn together by organizing meetups. You will work with Chapter Leaders and the Codecademy Product and Marketing teams to build and promote experiences that connect people at a local level all over the world, allowing them to learn with others and thus have a more well-rounded and effective learning experience.

WHAT YOU'LL DO:

Scale the Codecademy chapters program from dozens to thousands.

  • Support our Chapter Leaders as their primary point-of-contact.
  • Help Chapter Leaders grow their chapter membership and increase the number and quality of their meetups. Refine processes, create resources, and find new and innovative ways to support meetups.
  • Recruit and onboard new Chapter Leaders.
  • Reactivate dormant chapters.
  • Create strategic initiatives to build chapters in underrepresented areas.
  • Work with Chapter Leaders to create innovative ways to engage their community and grow their chapters.
  • Foster chapter-based communities to support cities, schools, and interest groups globally.

Measure the business impact of the chapters program. 

  • Tie chapter and meetup engagement to key product metrics such as acquisition, activation, conversion, and retention.
  • Work with the Data Science team to develop insights that can be used to evolve the chapters program and increase its impact on the business.
  • Work with our Marketing team to surface chapter stories and promote them broadly.
  • Share chapter stories and results with the company on a consistent basis.

WHAT YOU'LL NEED:

  • At least 2 years of experience as a Community Manager with a focus on building in-person or virtual communities to foster friendly collaboration.
  • You have verifiably excellent public speaking, written, and interpersonal communication skills.
  • A natural enthusiasm and interest in learning to code with Codecademy.
  • The ability to create and nurture a fun, imaginative, and collaborative space for Chapters Leaders and their chapter members.
  • The ability and penchant for project management; you take a project-driven approach to partnering with chapters on new initiatives and campaigns.
  • You’re a people-person and a connector; you have natural relationship-building skills, you're deeply empathetic, and you have a proven love for developing communities.
  • A firm understanding and ability to speak about technical products and initiatives.

WHAT WILL MAKE YOU STAND OUT:

  • Experience working with community platform tools such as Bevy, Discourse, and Discord.
  • Experience analyzing data with tools such as spreadsheets, SQL, and Looker.
  • Experience working in the technical education industry, ideally in a customer-facing role.
